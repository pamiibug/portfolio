01 Calling Intention

Brief

Creative work reaches deep into our intuitive and unconscious minds, into our hearts and feelings. Examine the ways in which your calling intention and pattern can be interpreted and represented in visual form, offering clarity, embedded meaning and understanding to the viewer.

Challenge

Visualize a highly abstract idea that source from your growing awareness of the deeper aspects of yourself in your own personal voice by exploring design elements, pattern, typography and diverse processes

Solution

Calling intention poster with calendar for the year 2023 22” by 28” MINIMUM final size; 4 color process
- 1st level of information: your calling
- 2nd level of information:
calendar (keep it to less than 1/4 of the format) exploring the circular/cyclical nature of time in your visualiztion

Pattern

Spirals signify movement and evolution as they start from the center and move outward. It can be seen throughout nature, from galaxies and weather patterns to every living organism's DNA. I chose the spiral as my pattern to visually represent my calling because I believe my purpose is to give back to humanity. The spiral symbolizes my inner calling that begins within and grows outward to reach other beings that I can share my consciousness with.
Calling Intention:

How can I nuture divine power within humanity so that we can balance artistry, purpose, and consciousness?

Sketches

  • Good use of pattern and scale
  • Too simple and a common design
  • Unique texture and visual approach
  • Needs asymmetric balance and the stipple design is time consuming
  • Good hierarchy; visually pleasing
  • Calling intention and spirals strategically positioned
  • Overall best balanced design

02 Building Awareness

Brief

We are stories. Everything humans do and experience evolves around some kind of story. It’s the way we contextualize information and experience to make it meaningful.
Design an orihon-style folding booklet visualizing the unique personality and character of your interview partner and their relationship to nature.

Challenge

Emphasis will be placed upon original concept development and the exploration of diverse processes, techniques and methods. You will be expected
to experiment and explore, thereby expanding on your visuaizing skills.

Solution

  • Orihon-style folding booklet
  • Design front and backside of the booklet with a final format of 4”by 4” folded, and 4” by 32” flat.
  • Use 4 color process (duotones, Calling intention poster with calendar for the year 2023 22” by 28” MINIMUM final size; 4 color process
interview and sketches

1. Who inspires you and Why? My uncle inspires me the most because even though his upbringing was rough, he maintained being a selfless, determined, and strong-willed man. 2. What color do you most resonate with and why? My favorite color is sky blue because it reminds me of the serene comfort of the sky and how it’s a reflection of the sea. 3. How do you access your intuition?I tend to be mindful instead of going with my first instinct. I weigh my pros an cons and try to analyze outcomes before making important decisions. 4. How has life changed you since Covid? Covid has taught me to be careful and mindful. I have to be aware of my hygiene and where I am going in order for me, my family, and roommates to be safe. It’s also taught me to go with the flow because there was an incident right before the pandemic where I was about to leave to Russia but then my dad had a really bad accident and so I had to cancel my plans. Next thing we know, a whole lockdown ensues. It would have meant I would’ve been stuck in Russia. Although my dad’s accident was horrible, coincidences like these has taught me to find balance in things. 5. What invitations are you offering to the world?Im a big advocate for protesting, volunteering, and designing with purpose. I’m also helping my Ukrainian friend by sending them supplies. 6. Tell me a transforming experience you’ve had in nature.I was driving to Southern California and stopped at a beach halfway there and just sat and reflected. I had just got a job acceptance and realized I usually never sit and acknowledge my own gratitude and blessings. I was able to slow down for a minute and contemplate not only this win for myself but admire the beautiful lifeforms of rock and ocean surrounding me.

Concept

Throughout my conversation with Elena, my interview partner, I learned that she has mastered  the balance between structure and following the flow of things. Flow and mindfulness were key words I took from our conversation and translated it to visual illustrations within abstract shapes and freeform waves. I wanted hues of blue to be the main color because it is Elena's favorite color and reminds her of how the sky meets the ocean. I hand drew the focus word "flow" in an orange text to give strong contrast.

The storytelling also comes into play when reading the booklet from left to right. Left being waves of turmoil and obstacles that she has overcome and learned from. Then the word “flow” being symbolic to how she now moves through life. And reaching towards the end of the booklet, the right has a calm and serene aspect to connect to her current mindfulness.

From this point in progress, I need to add an element to the back of the booklet to equally contrast the front. In order to do so I will add more abstract yet simpler shapes in an orange color to match the "flow" text.

03 Organizational Intentions

Brief

Entrepreneurship and branding: purpose, identity, web appearance, and artifact

Challenge

Grouped with 5 students with similar callings, design the intention and the brand for a fictitious non-profit organization that engages in the field of water:
  • water literacy (education)
  • water & social justice (gender equality)
  • water & culture (appreciation / art / imagination)
  • water & ecology (biodiversity / climate change / indigenous & emerging technologies)

Solution

Your group will design:- concept map of the field of water- name and intention of your organization
  • visual identity
  • online appearance
  • one artifact of your organization, eg: poster campaign, sculpture, product, etc.
  • ripple story (how the artifact influences individuals and culture)

Ideation

A common denominator within our calling intentions was igniting something from within ourselves in order to share and bring it forth within humanity. With our organization we wanted to spread the knowledge of the sustainability of algae and how it can be a catalyst in making world-changing architect and agriculture materials. Our organization uses interactive and bioluminescent algae exhibits to push for sustainable practices and represent our calling intention.
Calling Intention:

How can we share the sustainable properties of algae forests with humanity so that we empower conscious solutions to protect and restore precious ecosystems?

My Role

As a team, we had creative input in all aspects of the project. Individually, I was in charge of creating and designing the artifact which was an exterior and underground exhibit showcasing renewable resources and the vast variety of algae forms.
We chose our exhibit entrance to be dome shaped and made of algae solar panels that cultivates micro-algae to generate heat and biomass. Along the pathway to the dome will be algae sculptures made of algae cement. It was important that these features were in our artifact because they are natural closed loop renewable building materials that should be implemented world wide.  
As you enter the dome it leads to our Sea Beneath Boundaries exhibit where there is a live algae bioluminescent tank, life-size forest, and endless learning opportunities of how we can protect and restore ecosystems through algae.
