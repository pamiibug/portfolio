Advertising
The world’s best photo booth rentals – fun and interactive, with social media integration and amazing image quality.
About The Silicon Valley Business Journal runs a yearly challenge for local colleges to work with small businesses to create successful advertisement campaigns and an associated media buy and strategy using $25,000 to be used within the Journal. Our ad campaign was proudly awarded as "Best Presentation."
ChallengeI was teamed with five other designers to form a student design agency. The challenge was to develop an advertising campaign for a local business to be printed in the Silicon Valley Business Journal newspaper. The campaign was to be rolled out over the span of one calendar year, which we needed to plan using $25,000.
SolutionThe goal was to market to the Business Journal's demographic of mid to older age clientele and office associates while creating advertising that will strengthen the brand, easily display the photobooth's key features, and show Tapsnap's personality.

Discovery & Ideation

Each member of my agency pitched ideas for the posters, but my sketch took the most interest. Thus, I took on the role of designing the posters with another teammate while the rest strategized our media buy and designed our presentation. Our concept was to incorporate the office scene with the party life to be the most appealing to the middle-aged demographic. At first, we struggled with unbalanced hierarchy, distracting graphics, and it was hard to grasp what it was advertising.

Final Campaign

The final ad displays a lively and colorful design to display the message of joy that Tapsnap brings to its customers. There is a balanced hierarchy using contrast, negative space, and a clean color palette. Contrast between the negative space of the background and the scaled kiosk and users helps emphasize the product. We narrowed down the color palette to blue hues along with the pops of colors, but the colors having less visual hierarchy than our first iterations. Overall, there is complete balance because the pieces do not distract from each other, but rather work well together to present our message succinctly.     

Next, the solution for our media buy was to take into consideration the peak seasons of Tapsnap. Tapsnap hits its highest peaks during the holidays so we decided to split the visual advertisements between general use, holiday use, and corporate use. Our budgeting was planned to focus on purchasing 1/4 pages and  medium sizes over larger sizes because it would adhere to the budget best while still being noticeable in the journal.  

Lastly, a part of the budget was to either pick a sponsorship event or use digital advertising. We chose the sponsorship because Tapsnap, being woman and queer owned, wanted to reach beyond coporate world and build community relationships in the non profit field.

Our team won the judges over with “Best Presentation" award due to me and my team having a fluid, confident, and engaging speech and powerpoint. This was a great achievement for me because it was one of my first events where I was granted the gift to speak in front of a hundred people relating to my passion and hard work. This experience was my first time conducting a B2B transaction while working in an actual design agency.
