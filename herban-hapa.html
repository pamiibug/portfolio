Herban Hapa

Branding
Striving for positive economic and social change through culture, food, and Mana
About The Herban Hapa is a local bay area women owned tea herbalist company sustainably sourcing herbs from their very own family-owned farm in Hawaii. Using mana, a sacred Hawaiian ideology, they bring spiritual energy of power and strength into their business and products to create wellness.

Challenge

Herban Hapa are for people who care about where their food is being sourced and how it will ingest into their bodies. Herban Hapa believes in traditional herbal medicine dating back to ancient rituals. Therefore, creating a brand identity that conveys health, wisdom, and friendliness is critical to building a strong customer base.

Solution

Creating a brand identity that conveys transparency, good nature and reliability to build customer support. This can be done through design elements such as warm color palettes and clean, modern visuals.

Ideation

Herban Hapa's creators come from a mixed heritage background with Asian and Buddhist culture being a prevalent presence in their identity. They noted that Buddha's teachings are important in the work they do and wanted to see if it could be implemented in their brand. Along with herbalism comes a sort of "hippie" mentality — the 70's aesthetic comes naturally and ties in with a warm color palette.
The exciting part of brainstorming is taking all of the client's ideas and morphing them into a creative masterpiece. I took inspiration from 70's cartoon art and psychedelic designs to create fun and friendly sketches. The 2D animations of the 70's made an affectionate logo approach while the fluid typographic posters played with space.
Clean & Simple
We chose to go the clean and modern route with a slight edge of excitement within the text play.
The first iteration is the most bold and visually stimulating due to the wrapping of the text. Although it is an interesting design, the buddha aspect is difficult to visualize and the overall details may be lost at a smaller scale.
The best option of the two was the second choice because it had that fun psychedelic typography but still adhered to modern design choices with the simple contrasted outline of Buddha. The two design choices balance each other out.
