Stories for Solidarity

In partnership with Stories for Solidarity, a non-profit geared towards providing art opportunities for BIPOC folk in aid for greater change. During a time of despair from the George Floyd incident, I created two murals depicting solidarity as a message of hope. These murals were painted in the heart of downtown San Jose where marchers congressed. I was proudly part of a team that outreached to larger communities and gain traction for grants.

Community Mural

Covenant House California is a non-profit shelter providing sanctuary for youth overcoming homelessness and trafficking. I partnered with them to create an inspiring mural for the recreational art room. The mural was inspired by their commitment of community, meaningful change, and unconditional love. It features bright colors to bring joy, diversity to reflect their community, and symbolism of growth through butterflies and blooming flowers.

Stop AAPI Hate

At the height of Covid-19 the Asian demographic was targeted, hated, and ultimately murdered. I felt the urge to use illustration for social justice and equity awareness. Having just watched the film "Who Killed Vincent Chin," I was petrified to see an innocent life be slayed but amazed that it was a pivotal point for Asian Americans because it brought all Asian communities to form ACJ (American Citizens for Justice). This project was made using reports from the Stop AAPI Hate coalition and actual hate crimes reported by Asian Americans.

Illustration Exploration

An exploration of 2D, 3D, and 4D visual design using illustrative work. First, I explored 9 different illustration approaches using Procreate and Adobe Suite drawing inspiration from mainstream media, emotions, and trends. The most compelling visual used the contrasting words "sassy" and "sweet" and translated seamlessly to a 2D mural wall, 3D interactive maze installation, and a fun 4D visual.
